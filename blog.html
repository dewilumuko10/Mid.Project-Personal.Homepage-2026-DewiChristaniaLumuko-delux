Travel

≋ A Beautiful Day at Paal Beach

One of the most beautiful places I have ever visited is Paal Beach in Marinsow, Likupang, North Sulawesi ⚘

This beach is known for its clear blue water, soft white sand, and peaceful atmosphere. When I first arrived, I felt amazed by how beautiful and calm the place was. The sound of the waves and the fresh sea breeze made me feel relaxed and happy.

I visited this place with people close to me, and it became a very memorable experience. We spent time walking along the shore, enjoying the view, and taking photos together ◉. The scenery was so beautiful that every moment felt special.

“What I like most about Paal Beach is its natural beauty. It is not too crowded, so it feels peaceful and perfect for relaxing. The combination of the blue ocean, green hills, and bright sky creates a perfect view that is hard to forget.”

For me, this place is more than just a tourist destination. It is a place where I can feel calm, refresh my mind, and enjoy simple moments with the people I love ♡

I hope I can visit this beautiful beach again someday and create more wonderful memories there ✦

— A memory worth keeping ≋ ♡

Culture

◐ A Unique Encounter with the Manguni Owl

During my visit to Museum Pa'dior, I had the chance to see a very unique animal that is closely related to the culture of North Sulawesi — the Manguni bird, also known as the Sulawesi Scops Owl ◐

What made this experience special was that the bird was preserved and displayed inside the museum. Even though it was not alive, I could still clearly observe its physical features, such as its small body, sharp eyes, and detailed feathers.

The Manguni bird is not just an ordinary animal. In Minahasa culture, it has a deep meaning and is often associated with signs or messages. Because of this, the bird is considered special and respected by the local community.

“Seeing it in the museum made me realize how important it is to preserve both nature and culture. Museums like this help us learn more about animals that we might not see every day, while also keeping cultural values alive.”

For me, this was not just about seeing a bird, but also about understanding a part of my region's identity. It reminded me that North Sulawesi has many unique things that we should appreciate and protect ♡

This experience was simple, but meaningful, and it made my visit even more memorable ✦

— Preserving culture, one story at a time ◐

Heritage

❀ The Beauty of Tontemboan Language

As someone who comes from South Minahasa, I feel proud to be connected to one of the local languages called Tontemboan ❀

Tontemboan is one of the traditional languages spoken by the people of Minahasa, especially in South Minahasa. It is not just a way of communication, but also an important part of cultural identity and heritage.

Growing up, I have heard this language in daily conversations, especially from older family members. Even though I may not use it all the time, I still understand how meaningful it is. The language carries history, values, and traditions that have been passed down from generation to generation.

In today’s modern world, many young people prefer to use Indonesian or even foreign languages. Because of this, local languages like Tontemboan are slowly being used less often. This makes me realize how important it is for us to preserve and appreciate our own culture.

“For me, Tontemboan is more than just a language. It represents where I come from and who I am. It connects me to my roots, my family, and my identity.”

I hope that in the future, more young people will learn and continue to use Tontemboan, so that this beautiful language will never be forgotten ✦

— Proud of my roots ❀ ♡
